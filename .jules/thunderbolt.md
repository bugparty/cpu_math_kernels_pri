## 2024-05-18 - [AVX2 Softmax with Range Reduction]
**Learning:** Computing `exp` in AVX2 without Intel SVML requires range reduction for reasonable accuracy and performance. Simply using a Taylor polynomial fails on large negative numbers (frequent in softmax after subtracting `max`). Decomposing `exp(x)` into `2^n * 2^f` allows a short (5th-degree) polynomial to approximate `2^f` accurately. Also, normalizing by multiplying by the inverse sum instead of dividing element-wise significantly increases throughput.
**Evidence:** The scalar `softmax_naive` computes `max`, `exp`, and `sum` individually yielding ~0.57 GFLOP/s. By vectorizing the `max` and `sum` reductions horizontally and using the AVX2 `exp` approximation, throughput jumps to ~4.27 GFLOP/s (7.5x speedup).
**Action:** When calculating `exp` or other transcendental functions across architectures, always implement a range-reduction approach (e.g. `2^(x * log2(e))`) instead of a raw Maclaurin/Taylor series to avoid underflow/overflow/inaccuracy, and clamp lower bounds for operations like softmax where small `exp` values effectively approach zero.
